# Thirteen open problems at the intersection of topology, types, and physics

**The formal verification of fundamental physics remains profoundly incomplete.** Across all thirteen questions examined here — spanning tight-binding theory, quantum gravity, scrambling dynamics, emergent spacetime, measurement theory, algebraic structure, and thermodynamics — the same structural pattern recurs: rigorous mathematical machinery exists for each domain, partial formalizations have been achieved in proof assistants and categorical frameworks, but the critical physical interpretive bridges connecting these formalisms to observable reality remain unverified conjectures. Of the thirteen questions, exactly two yield proven theorems (entanglement = wormhole in 3d TQFT; Adams' obstruction for Cayley-Dickson descent), three yield semi-rigorous bounds (MSS, scrambling, complexity growth), and the remainder expose open problems ranging from decades-old to genuinely novel. What follows is a systematic assessment of the strongest literature, identified gaps, and formal verifiability for each.

---

## 1–2. Harrison's d⁻² is an approximation, not a theorem, and ER=EPR is proven only in 3d

**Harrison scaling (Q1).** Walter Harrison's celebrated d⁻² law for tight-binding hopping integrals — V_{ll'm} = η_{ll'm} · ℏ²/md² — arises from matching LCAO band structures to free-electron theory at high-symmetry points. The **1/d² dependence inherits directly from the kinetic energy of a free electron confined to a box of size d**, making it a dimensional-analysis result, not a lattice-dependent one. The honeycomb geometry of graphene enters only through Slater-Koster angular factors and neighbor counting, not through the radial exponent. Piermarocchi et al. (1995) demonstrated clear deviations from d⁻² in strained Si and Ge, and modern parameterizations (Goodwin-Skinner-Pettifor) use individual fitted exponents. **No formal proof constrains the exponent to exactly −2 on any lattice.** The true overlap integrals between atomic orbitals decay exponentially at large distances; the power law is an intermediate-distance approximation. No machine-checked formalization of tight-binding theory exists in any proof assistant.

**ER=EPR (Q2).** The strongest result is **Baez and Vicary's 2014 theorem**: in 3d TQFT, the creation of an entangled particle-antiparticle pair IS the formation of a wormhole — they are literally the same cobordism. The type signature of an entangled pair, in Abramsky-Coecke categorical quantum mechanics, is a morphism **η: I → A ⊗ B** in a dagger compact closed category that does not factor as η₁ ⊗ η₂. In the cobordism interpretation, this is a pair-of-pants surface — a handle attachment to space. Sati and Schreiber (2023) have advanced the formalization using Linear Homotopy Type Theory (LHoTT), where entangled states arise as sections of dependent linear types that cannot be decomposed as products.

The critical distinction question — can topological invariants separate entanglement from wormholes? — has a sharp answer **in 3d TQFT: no**, they are provably indistinguishable. In 4d, the ER=EPR conjecture remains informal. The nLab identifies a "typing error" between quantum and classical gravity that has not been resolved. Topological entanglement entropy γ = log D (Kitaev-Preskill, Levin-Wen 2006) detects long-range entanglement but whether this is equivalent to geometric connection in 4d remains open. Fields and Glazebrook (2024) recently argued that ER=EPR is "operationally provable" without full AdS/CFT, but this work awaits community validation.

## 3–4. Scrambling obeys proven logarithmic bounds; Volovik's N₃ gives kinematics but not dynamics

**Scrambling bounds (Q3).** The Maldacena-Shenker-Stanford bound λ_L ≤ 2πk_BT/ℏ constrains the Lyapunov exponent of quantum chaotic systems under plausible physical assumptions — it is semi-rigorous, not a full theorem. The more powerful result is **Lashkari et al.'s 2013 proof** using Lieb-Robinson techniques: any Hamiltonian with finite-norm local terms requires scrambling time t* ≥ (β/2π) log S, a rigorous logarithmic lower bound. Haferkamp et al. (2022, *Nature Physics*) proved that quantum circuit complexity grows linearly before saturating at exponential values, confirming the Brown-Susskind conjecture. Aharonov and Touati (2018) proved Ω(log n) circuit depth lower bounds for preparing topological states, directly bounding how fast topology can reorganize.

**No formal connection to dependent type theory or proof complexity exists.** This is a genuinely open frontier. The suggestive analogy — scrambling redistributes information globally, analogous to global proof reorganization — has not been formalized. Atkey (2024) showed dependent type theory can capture complexity classes (P, NP, PSPACE) via resource monoids, and Susskind's complexity-volume conjecture connects black hole dynamics to PSPACE. But a rigorous mapping between MSS-type bounds and type-theoretic bounds remains entirely unestablished.

**Volovik's emergent spacetime (Q4).** The minimal requirement is **a single topological invariant N₃ = ±1** — the momentum-space winding number protecting Weyl/Fermi points (codimension 3 nodes). When N₃ ≠ 0, the Atiyah-Bott-Shapiro construction guarantees that the low-energy Hamiltonian expands linearly in Dirac matrices, producing emergent tetrads e^a_μ, an emergent metric g^{μν} = η^{ab}e^a_μ e^b_ν, emergent gauge fields (from the Weyl point position in momentum space), and emergent Lorentz invariance. Nissinen and Volovik (2018–2022) have demonstrated concrete realizations including torsional gravitational anomalies in Weyl superfluids.

**However, N₃ gives the kinematics of GR, not the dynamics.** The emergent equations of motion are hydrodynamic, not the Einstein field equations. Volovik emphasizes that Einstein-Cartan theory (with torsion) is more natural than standard GR, and the Einstein equations emerge only when the Lorentz-violating energy scale far exceeds the Planck energy. No rigorous proof exists that {N₃} is both necessary and sufficient for emergent GR, and deriving Einstein's dynamics from the microscopic theory remains the central unsolved problem of the program.

## 5–6. Non-disturbing measurement is typeable in principle; persistent homology cannot uniquely reconstruct symmetry-breaking history

**Non-disturbing measurement (Q5).** The framework for formally typing a non-disturbing topological measurement exists in Schreiber, Myers, and Riley's Linear Homotopy Type Theory (LHoTT). The dependent pair would take the form:

> NonDisturbingMeasurement := Σ(ℰ : Instrument, T : TopologicalObservable, pf : ∀ρ. T(post(ℰ(ρ))) = T(ρ))

The physical mechanism for non-disturbance is **gap protection**: topological invariants take integer values, and measurement back-action below the bulk gap energy cannot change the topological class. Kong et al. (2016) experimentally measured Chern numbers using single NV centers; Lysne et al. (2023) proposed heterodyne cavity measurements of Berry curvature. However, Snizhko, Romito, and Gefen (2020) proved that measurement-induced back-action itself has topological structure — varying measurement strength triggers topological transitions in geometric phases. **The specific proof term for gap-protected non-disturbance has not been constructed in LHoTT.** The pieces exist (quantum verification framework, QND theory, topological measurement protocols) but their integration into a single verified dependent pair is an open problem. A key subtlety: Chern numbers are not standard quantum observables (operator eigenvalues) but derived quantities computed from the ground-state manifold, requiring extension of standard QND formalism.

**Topological archaeology (Q6).** Persistent homology detects phase transitions (Donato et al. 2016), extracts interpretable order parameters (Cole et al. 2021), and even distinguishes the direction of transitions through asymmetric structural heterogeneity signatures (Membrillo Solis et al. 2021). For cosmological structure, Biagetti et al. (2024) showed PH probes non-local correlations that **cannot be altered by late-time physics** — a powerful result for topological archaeology.

**But unique reconstruction is provably impossible from PH alone.** Leygonie and Henselman-Petrusek (2022, 2024) formally studied the inverse problem: the fiber PH⁻¹(D) — the set of all filtrations producing barcode D — is generically a positive-dimensional polyhedral complex, sometimes with non-trivial topology (even Möbius bands). Multiple distinct symmetry-breaking sequences can produce identical persistence diagrams. Physical constraints (energy minimization, subgroup chains, Kibble-Zurek defect scaling) may collapse the fiber, and multi-parameter persistence or persistent Laplacians carry strictly more information. But no theorem establishes that any augmented persistent homology framework suffices for unique reconstruction of a symmetry-breaking cascade.

## 7–9. Groundedness is semi-decidable, minimal algebras are hyperfinite type III₁, and some infinities in physics are irreplaceable

**Epistemic audit (Q7).** Proof checking is decidable: given a formal proof term in any fixed type theory, verification terminates in bounded time. This is implemented in every proof assistant (Lean 4's `Decidable` typeclass, Agda's `Dec` type). **Proof existence is only semi-decidable** — if a proof exists, systematic search will find it; if not, the search may run forever. Gödel's first incompleteness theorem guarantees that no decidable predicate can perfectly classify all statements as "grounded" vs. "speculative" in any system containing arithmetic. The practical resolution is a four-level classification: Verified (with checked proof term), AxiomDependent (tracking dependency), Conjectured (human-assigned evidence), and Speculative (default). The transition from Speculative to Verified is semi-decidable; verification of the Verified tag is fully decidable. This is **the strongest achievable result** given fundamental logical constraints.

**Minimal algebraic structure (Q8).** The Haag-Kastler axioms define the minimal framework: a net of C*-algebras over spacetime regions satisfying isotony, microcausality, Poincaré covariance, the spectrum condition, and vacuum existence. **Yngvason's remarkable result** (2004): all local algebras in well-behaved QFTs are generically isomorphic to the unique hyperfinite type III₁ von Neumann factor. The physics lies entirely in how these identical algebras are embedded relative to one another. DHR superselection theory shows that gauge groups and particle statistics are reconstructible from the observable net alone — they are derived, not fundamental.

String theory additionally requires vertex operator algebras, modular invariance, extra dimensions, and (usually) supersymmetry. **No formal proof exists that string theory's structure is strictly larger than what observables require**, because neither the Standard Model has been rigorously constructed as a Haag-Kastler net (this is essentially the Clay Millennium Prize problem for Yang-Mills), nor does string theory have a complete non-perturbative definition. A 2025 Lean 4 formalization achieved end-to-end verification of free bosonic QFT satisfying Osterwalder-Schrader axioms — the first such result, but still far from the interacting Standard Model.

**Infinity in physics (Q9).** The substitution **provably fails** in at least five contexts:

- **Phase transitions** require the thermodynamic limit (partition functions of finite systems are analytic — no discontinuities)
- **Haag's theorem** demonstrates unitarily inequivalent representations that exist only in infinite dimensions
- **Chiral fermions** cannot be faithfully discretized (Nielsen-Ninomiya theorem)
- **Quantum anomalies** arise from infinite degrees of freedom and encode essential physics (π⁰ → γγ)
- **Spontaneous symmetry breaking** requires infinite volume (finite-volume tunneling prevents true degeneracy)

Conversely, lattice QCD succeeds spectacularly as a finite computational framework; constructive QFT rigorously constructs interacting theories in d < 4 (Glimm-Jaffe); and Schreiber-Shulman's cohesive HoTT handles ∞-stacks natively via synthetic means. The Isham-Butterfield-Döring topos program replaces Boolean truth values with Heyting algebras but has not eliminated infinite-dimensional Hilbert spaces. **No ultrafinitist formulation of the Standard Model exists**, and the 2025 Columbia conference on ultrafinitism concluded that finitary physics, while conceptually appealing, cannot yet reproduce the Standard Model's predictions.

## 10–11. Adams' theorem provides the obstruction for Cayley-Dickson; gravity is typeable as a 2-connection but dynamics must be supplied

**Cayley-Dickson descent (Q10).** The property cascade is precisely characterized: ℝ → ℂ loses self-conjugacy; ℂ → ℍ loses **commutativity**; ℍ → 𝕆 loses **associativity**; 𝕆 → 𝕊 loses **alternativity, composition, and division** (zero divisors appear). The first four algebras support Hopf fibrations (S¹→S³→S², S³→S⁷→S⁴, S⁷→S¹⁵→S⁸), parallelizable unit spheres, and H-space structures. **Adams' 1960 theorem** — simplified via K-theory by Adams-Atiyah in 1966 — proves these are the only maps of Hopf invariant one, constituting the definitive topological obstruction. The proof uses Adams operations ψ^k on complex K-theory: consistency requires 2^m | (3^m − 1), which holds only for m = 1, 2, 4.

The connection to **Bott periodicity** is deep: Clifford algebra periodicity Cliff(n+8) ≅ Cliff(n) ⊗ ℝ(16) underlies the period-8 repetition of KO-theory homotopy groups, and the division algebras ℝ, ℂ, ℍ appear explicitly in the Bott periodicity sequence. Moreno (1998) proved the norm-1 sedenion zero divisors are homeomorphic to the exceptional Lie group **G₂** — confirmed geometrically in a 2024 paper showing isometry with a naturally reductive metric. Buchholtz and Rijke formalized the Cayley-Dickson construction in HoTT but noted that iteration beyond 𝕆 requires associativity, which fails. Whether sedenions can be "rescued" as monoid objects in higher fusion categories (as suggested by the nLab) remains an open conjecture.

**Gravity as morphism (Q11).** Three precise categorical formulations exist. First, **Rosenstock-Barrett-Weatherall (2015)** proved that the category of Einstein algebras (Geroch 1972) is categorically dual to the category of relativistic spacetimes, with morphisms preserving empirical structure. Second, **Baez and Wise (2014)** showed GR can be rewritten as a higher gauge theory: the gravitational field is a **2-connection for the teleparallel 2-group**, packaging coframe, flat connection, and torsion as a single categorical object. This is the most literal "gravity as morphism" result. Third, **Schreiber (2013)** formulated gravity as a cocycle in GL(n)-twisted differential cohomology within a cohesive ∞-topos, where gauge transformations are morphisms between cocycles.

All three recover the Einstein field equations — but by reformulating the variational principle within categorical language, not by deriving dynamics from categorical axioms alone. **No one has derived Einstein's equations from purely morphism-based axioms without importing differential-geometric or variational content.** The categorical frameworks illuminate the structural content of GR (Kostecki writes the equations in synthetic differential geometry; Raptis derives vacuum Einstein equations on finitary causal-set sheaves) but the specific form of the Einstein-Hilbert action must still be supplied. This remains a major open problem.

## 12–13. Entropy production formally quantifies time-asymmetry but not temporal "flow," and topological phases occupy the boundary between thermodynamic laws

**Time as dissipative trace (Q12).** The **Connes-Rovelli thermal time hypothesis** (1994), grounded in the Tomita-Takesaki theorem, derives a one-parameter modular automorphism group from any faithful state on a von Neumann algebra, interpreted as physical time flow. The mathematics is rigorous; the physical interpretation is not. Rovelli and Smerlak (2011) derived the Tolman-Ehrenfest effect from the single principle that temperature equals the ratio of thermal time to proper time — the TTH's most concrete prediction.

The **strongest proven connection** between entropy production and time is the Kawai-Parrondo-Van den Broeck identity (2007): ⟨W_diss⟩ = kT · D_KL(ρ_F ‖ ρ̃_R), where entropy production exactly equals the Kullback-Leibler divergence between forward and time-reversed processes. This is an exact theorem, not an approximation, valid for classical and quantum systems. The Crooks fluctuation theorem and Jarzynski equality provide complementary exact identities.

**Three gaps remain unbridged.** First, entropy production quantifies time-asymmetry (a proven result) but not temporal "flow" (an interpretive claim). Second, no theory singles out phase boundaries as special for time — the phase-boundary entropy production literature addresses engineering thermodynamics without connecting to temporal experience. Third, the Connes-Rovelli TTH faces serious objections: circularity (defining KMS states presupposes dynamics), the classical limit problem (abelian algebras give trivial modular flow), and no extension to the non-equilibrium conditions that characterize real phase boundaries. Weberszpil and Sotolongo-Costa (2026) proposed a unified "entropy as clock" framework, but it remains a parametric ansatz. **No proof-assistant formalization of any entropy-time relationship exists** beyond basic gas laws.

**The boundary between the second and third laws (Q13).** This regime is physically real. **Spin ice** (Dy₂Ti₂O₇, Ho₂Ti₂O₇) exhibits extensive residual entropy S ≈ R[ln 2 − ½ ln(3/2)] at T → 0, with an emergent Coulomb phase and magnetic monopole excitations — though Pomaranski et al. (2013) argued this may reflect kinetic trapping rather than true equilibrium. **Quantum spin liquids** remain disordered at T = 0 through quantum fluctuations, with gapless varieties (U(1) spin liquids with spinon Fermi surfaces) potentially carrying non-zero entropy density. **Topological phases** at zero temperature have zero extensive entropy but non-zero **topological entanglement entropy** γ = ln D, where D is the total quantum dimension of the anyon theory.

Matter in this regime exhibits no conventional order (no broken symmetry, no local order parameter), topological order characterized by ground-state degeneracy dependent on manifold topology, fractional/anyonic excitations, long-range quantum entanglement, and emergent gauge structure. The "phase space" is parametrized not by thermodynamic variables but by topological invariants: Chern numbers, quantum dimensions, and modular data of topological quantum field theories. The third law in its strong Planck form (S → 0) holds for the extensive part but misses these topological corrections, which are subleading in system size yet physically essential — encoding the difference between trivial and topological ground states.

## Conclusion: a landscape of semi-formalization

Across all thirteen questions, a coherent picture emerges. The mathematical infrastructure for formal verification of fundamental physics — dependent type theory, cohesive HoTT, categorical quantum mechanics, AQFT — has reached remarkable sophistication. Lean 4 can now verify free QFT axioms; LHoTT can express quantum measurement as dependent type computation; Baez-Wise teleparallel gravity lives naturally in 2-categories; Adams' theorem provides machine-checkable obstruction theory for division algebras.

Yet the **interpretive bridges** — the claims that modular flow IS time, that entangled pairs ARE wormholes in 4d, that Weyl point topology IMPLIES Einstein dynamics, that persistent homology CAN reconstruct symmetry-breaking history — remain stubbornly resistant to formalization. The fundamental obstacle is not computational but conceptual: these bridges cross from pure mathematics into physical semantics, where Gödel-type limits, the measurement problem, and the absence of rigorous interacting 4d QFT construction (the Millennium Prize problem) create irreducible gaps. The most productive near-term targets for formal verification are **Adams' obstruction theory** (already partially formalized in HoTT), the **Kawai-Parrondo-Van den Broeck identity** (exact theorem amenable to proof-assistant encoding), and **gap-protected non-disturbance of topological measurements** (where LHoTT provides the right language and the physics suggests a clear proof strategy). The deepest open problem cutting across all thirteen questions is whether categorical/type-theoretic axioms can ever *derive* physical dynamics — Einstein's equations, the Standard Model action — rather than merely reformulate them.
