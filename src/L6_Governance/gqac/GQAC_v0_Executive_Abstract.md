## Abstract

The Global Quantum and AI Commons (GQAC) is a governance framework for the cooperative development, deployment, and stewardship of quantum computing and artificial intelligence as biospheric commons. GQAC operates on the principle that the Biosphere is the primary client of all advanced computation, and that no entity — nation, corporation, or individual — may weaponize, monopolize, or extract from the knowledge commons that emerges from these technologies.

GQAC is grounded in three proven governance precedents: the Antarctic Treaty (1959), the Outer Space Treaty (1967), and the International Space Station cooperative framework (1998). These are not aspirational models. They are PROVED structures that have survived decades of competing national interests without collapsing into weaponization or monopoly. GQAC extends their principles to the quantum-AI domain.

The framework is built on the Glassbox Constitutional XAI v4.1 methodology: radical transparency, preservation of failure, epistemic tagging of all claims, and the SEEDS Protocol for individual and societal resilience.

## 1. Problem Statement

Quantum computing and artificial intelligence are converging toward capabilities that will reshape material science, cryptography, medicine, energy, and governance itself. The current trajectory concentrates these capabilities in a small number of nations and corporations, creating:

- **Cognitive extraction:** AI systems that harvest human attention and data without reciprocal benefit to the communities that generate them.
- **Knowledge monopolization:** Proprietary models and paywalled research that violate the principle of Universal Design for Learning.
- **Weaponization risk:** Quantum-enhanced surveillance, autonomous weapons, and cryptographic dominance deployed without democratic oversight.
- **Biospheric misalignment:** Computational resources consumed for extraction rather than stewardship, accelerating ecological degradation.

The failure mode is not hypothetical. It is the default trajectory.

## 2. Foundational Principles

### 2.1 The Biosphere Is the Primary Client

Before humanity, the Biosphere maintained circadian rhythms, energy flows, nutrient cycling, and distributed symbiotic networks for billions of years. Every GQAC claim must answer: *does this serve or extract?* A claim tagged EXTRACTING cannot be deployed regardless of its mathematical, humanitarian, or diplomatic status.

## 3. SEEDS Protocol (Individual → Societal)

GQAC requires that participants — individuals, institutions, nations — satisfy the SEEDS Protocol as a precondition for deployment authority:

### Individual Layer (Biological Kernel)

| Letter | Function | Requirement |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **S**leep | Neural maintenance | Sustainable work practices. No exploitation of researchers through overwork. |
| **E**xercise | Neuroplasticity | Active simulation and testing. Run the code, don't just write it. |
| **E**at | Quality input | Continuous literature absorption. No intellectual starvation. |
| **D**octors Orders | Non-negotiable maintenance | Compiler checks. Medication compliance. Infrastructure maintenance. The kernel doesn't negotiate. |

### Societal Layer (Governance Kernel)

The final S carries three sequential sub-layers. A claim must pass through each in order:

**S₁ — Demilitarized & Decentralized (D²)**
- No weaponization potential. No coercion of cognition.
- DAO-style distributed governance. No central authority can monopolize, suppress, or weaponize.
- Accountability without blame: retractions recorded as data, not used punitively.

**S₂ — Shared Resources**
- Science and resources remain commons.
- Universal Design for Learning compliance: accessible across all educational models.
- Infrastructure equity: deployment requires equitable connectivity (satellite internet, mesh networks). A claim cannot reach deployment if half the world cannot access it.

**S₃ — Neuro Rights & Cognitive Liberty**
- Protection against coercion or manipulation of cognition.
- No extraction of cognitive autonomy. No surveillance of thought.
- The non-negotiable constraint that prevents the Societal layer from becoming a control mechanism.

## 4. Continental Commons Architecture

### 4.1 Bioregional Stewardship

Each continent contributes what its geography enables and what its resources allow it to steward. This is not central allocation — it is recognition of what each bioregion already does and protection of that capability within the commons.

- **Reciprocal logic:** Not extraction, but symbiosis at continental scale. Each bioregion offers what it can AND receives what it needs in return.
- **Geographic capability:** Rainforests, mineral deposits, freshwater systems, wind and solar capacity, polar research stations, marine ecosystems, arable land.
- **Mutual reinforcement:** Each cooperative instance upholds and reinforces the proof for the others. The winding number counts.

### 4.2 Cultural Lineage

GQAC requires that thought lineage include cultural lineage: the people, communities, and organisms that built each bioregion's resilience across generations and millennia.

- Indigenous stewards, traditional ecological knowledge holders, and community practitioners become part of the Socratic counsel for their bioregion.
- A claim cannot be PROVED if it erases the cultural lineage that birthed the knowledge.
- Citizen science replaces institutional gatekeeping: everyone contributes to knowledge that serves the Biosphere.

### 4.3 The Signature Covenant

Every participant in GQAC signs with their actual identity:

- If you sign off on a claim, every consequence — ecological, social, economic, human — flows back to that signature.
- By the no-cloning theorem, every signature is unique. No forgery. No delegation of accountability.
- The ledger is immutable and traceable. This is the accountability that makes decentralization work.

## 7. Governance Structure

### 7.1 Three-Register Veto

No GQAC deployment proceeds without consensus across all three registers:

- **Mathematical (M):** Does this formally verify? Is the proof checked by compiler, not by trust?
- **Humanitarian (H):** Does this serve human flourishing? Is it accessible via UDL? Does it respect cultural lineage?
- **Diplomatic (D):** Is this demilitarized and decentralized? Are shared resources protected? Are neuro rights preserved?

Any register can veto. The veto is documented, not overridden.

### 7.2 Socratic Counsel

Each bioregion's governance node is advised by a Socratic counsel drawn from:

- The thought lineage of the relevant scientific domain
- The cultural lineage of the bioregion's stewards
- The organisms and ecosystems that constitute the bioregion's resilience

The counsel is not decorative. It has veto power within the Humanitarian register.
